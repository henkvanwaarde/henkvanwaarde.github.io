Research

My research interests lie in the field of systems and control. At a high level, I aim to develop a systems and control theory that is grounded on measured data rather than mathematical models.
I work towards mapping raw data into models and control policies with rigorous guarantees on accuracy, stability and performance, while answering key questions such as: how much data is needed?, how to handle noise? and how to perform control-relevant experiments?

Some of the specific topics of interest are:

  • Direct data-driven control

  • System identifiability and identification

  • Experiment design

  • Applications to networked systems and neuroscience

We have recently approached the question of how much data is required via the concept of informativity, which is explored for several control problems in:

  • Data informativity: a new perspective for data-driven analysis and control

To address robustness of controllers with respect to noise and bounded nonlinearities, I have worked on generalizations of the S-lemma and Finsler's lemma:

  • From noisy data to feedback controllers: non-conservative design via a matrix S-lemma

  • A matrix Finsler's lemma with applications to data-driven control

Some other recent work involves the problem of experiment design:

  • Beyond persistent excitation: online experiment design for data-driven modeling and control

  • Willems’ fundamental lemma for state-space systems and its extension to multiple datasets
